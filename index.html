Why These Simulators Matter for Deputy Head of Data Role

As Deputy Head of Data & Solutions, you own three critical responsibilities: (1) enabling the business with data-driven insights, (2) optimizing infrastructure costs, and (3) ensuring regulatory compliance. These three simulators demonstrate how to approach each challenge strategically.

Business Case Development

These tools show the CFO/Board that you don't just "do technology"—you quantify business impact. Each simulator models costs, risks, and multi-year financial impact with sensitivity analysis, showing what assumptions drive ROI.

Cross-Functional Leadership

Data integration isn't just a data problem—it's an operations + compliance + technology problem. Cloud migration isn't just infrastructure—it's a financial + risk + organizational change problem. These simulators show you think holistically across silos.

Risk Management

Each simulator includes compliance checkpoints (FINMA, GDPR, data residency), risk gates, and sensitivity analysis. Shows you're not just chasing technical elegance but protecting the organization.

For CFO/Board: Financial impact, ROI, cost optimization
For CRO: Compliance roadmap, risk gates, regulatory readiness
For COO: Process improvements, timeline management, team ramp-up
For CTO: Technical architecture, cloud strategy, tool selection

Technical Details & Data Sourcing

Why These Simulators Are Realistic (But Not Exact)

These models are built on:

  • Industry benchmarks: Swiss private banking typical costs, timelines, and adoption rates
  • Public sources: AWS pricing models, cloud migration best practices from AWS/Azure whitepapers
  • Process standards: Typical bank customer onboarding flows (based on FINMA guidance, industry practice)
  • Regulatory knowledge: DORA, GDPR, FINMA requirements are accurately modeled

What They DON'T Include (And Why)

These simulators are intentionally simplified for clarity and strategic insight. They don't include:

  • Julius Baer's proprietary cost data (which I don't have access to)
  • Specific legacy system details (e.g., mainframe contract terms)
  • Current team structure or capacity constraints
  • Detailed technical architecture decisions (e.g., exact cloud provider choice)

In a real Deputy Head role, these would be customized with actual data during Month 1 planning.

How to Interpret the Numbers

Use these simulators as:

  • ✅ Framework for thinking about the problem
  • ✅ Structure for questions to ask stakeholders ("What are our actual data quality metrics?")
  • ✅ Proof of concept that you can build models and analyze scenarios
  • ❌ NOT as Julius Baer's actual baseline or approved roadmap
